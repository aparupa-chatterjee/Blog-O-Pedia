Mandir of Mandya


In the heartland of Southern India, lies the city of sugar, or Sakkare Nadu, the former being the English translation of the later. This, of course, is due to Mandya, yet another name for our destination, being one of the largest sugarcane producers in all of India. The rivers Kaveri, Shimsha, Hemavati and Lokapavani inundate the tropical wetlands that make this fertile land possible. Throughout the history of India the rich culture and heritage of this city has been built up as it exchanged being under the various kingdoms of ancient India, like the Ganga dynasty, the Cholas, Vijaynagara, Hoysala and finally under the rule of Tipu Sultan and the British. Of course, the most famous of all is the magnificent temple of Saumakeshava, built in the 12th century A.D. This temple was the brainchild of the Hoysala rulers. The majestic grandeur of this temple reflecting a part tale of flamboyance, which as such, has been declared a monument of national importance by the Indian Archaeological Society. Another less famous but equally impressive outing is to the Cheluvanarayana Swamy temple. The impeccable stone carvings on the pillars are quite the sight to behold before you can retire to the impressive local delicacies of backwaters Karnataka.

-Suburn Roy

Forts of Mandvi

Amidst the grand Rann Kutch of the land of the Gujjars, bordered by the Arabian Sea, lies the town steeped in years of medieval Indian history. Hidden among the mirages, lies Mandvi. The city once being the summer retreat of the Maharao of the Kutch kingdom itself. The grand forted city exudes a mystical charm of old through the ground itself. The cool Arabian Sea being the perfect retreat after a day full of soaking in the delights dotted through the land. One key sight to witness would be the fort itself, which, unfortunately is undergoing gradual destruction, but the remaining walls still tell tales of Royals walking through and the heroic Talwars that once called this place their home. The shipbuilding industry is another huge part of Mandvi’s economy, and the timber ships still intrigue visitors to this very day. And of course, one can’t forget the food, and don’t you worry for Mandvi has you all set and covered. Other than the delicious Guajarati delicacies is the famous Special Dabeli. It’s a bit like a burger of yore, but with spicy mashed potatoes lined with a lip smacking blend of classic spices from India. For those keen about the grand history of India, Mandvi will, without a shadow of a doubt, be a delight like nothing else.

-Aniket Sahoo

Fair of Tilwara

Around the month of March, after the Holi festival, this place turns into an equestrian paradise. As hundreds of tents line up on the vast sandy dunes of the Thar, extravagant and exquisite breeds of indigenous horses lay awaiting prospecting visitors. Tilwara is a pivotal event for India’s horse calendar and culture. The event consumes the area of the seasonal Luni dry riverbed, adjacent to the small village of Tilwara. The fair is a combination of hoof beats, dusty whirlwinds and tents. Bovines, equines and cameline inhabit the arid fairgrounds. Exhibitors, horse owners, trainers, and traders place tents strategically around the perimeter of the riverbed, allowing easy navigation through the menagerie of people and animals. The famous Tilwara Horse Show is slated for the last three days of the fair, attracting horse owners from every corner of India. With nearly 3,000 majestic Indian horses represented, the noble steeds come together for an elaborate concentration of intrigue, beauty and demonstration. The aristocratic, indigenous horses of India are exalted, showcasing their royal heritage, smooth gait and grand disposition. For any prospecting visitor that has the time, this little unassuming place can turn out to be an amazing spot to make a memory of a lifetime amidst the dunes and the hoof beats of the equines and the starlit Rajasthan desert.

-Risabh Jha

Delights of Bengal

Bengal, or more formally, West Bengal, is one of the longest historically consistent states in the entire Indian subcontinent sharing a very distinct culture marking it unique from the rest of the Indian nation. Royal Rajasthan

This place literally translates to, the place of the Rajas. This ancient princely state is home to a large chunk of India’s storied history, and many kings and kingdoms that fabled the chapters of India’s history called this dry desert their home. Rajhastan is quite unlike anywhere else in India, through the dry dunes shifting about the sun tirelessly throughout the year lies a wealth of beauty and culture. The mystical aura that permeates through the desert dunes that call forth weary travellers into the night full of milk spilled over a dark, cloudless sky. Lay there, in front of a fire, with some good old Royal roast going on while the guys drum up some beats and you will be whole. Amidst the unique and distinct culture and architecture, the storied past of Rajas and Ranis, the brutal and bloody battles that stained the almost snow like sands, you will find an irrevocable and insatiable wanderlust that will draw you in and not let you go.

The Capital

The Sultanate Capital of yore still stands as the grand metropolitan capital of the great Indian nation. Being one of the world’s largest cities and India’s largest Union Territory is quite the honor, but Delhi does it all with an ease that can only come from years and years of experience at being the top city. From the times of the Mughal Raj, this North Indian city has been a bastion of culture and heritage. A mingling of urban and of old. Such is evident from the countless minor monuments from old times dotted around a land equally filled to the brim with the best of India, perhaps second only to Mumbai. Then there is the exquisite grandeur of places like the Red Fort, the Raj Bhawan, and of course, a bit far into the Agra area, Taj Mahal itself, in all it’s extravagant glory. Memories of times past forever etched into the white marbled walls of it’s facade. For a traveller, a place like Delhi might at first seem cliche, but look into it, meld into the culture and you will, without fail, lose yourself into a fascinating mix of culture and modernity that won’t be found anywhere else.

Wilderness of kerala

Along the southern edges of India lies one of India’s hidden jewels. The backwater state of Kerala. On the face of it, Kerala is known for being a highly progressive and altruistic state that mixes in the best of both worlds, and Kerala is damn good at it too. Take your time and rest a classic backwater floating hotel and experience Kerala like it’s meant to be. You will have an experience quite unlike anything else. The tropical climate, the palm and coconut trees . It dotted the coasts, the impressive cuisine that has every possible menu more than covered and the serenity of the tropical paradise which still retains the old charm that facilitated it’s growth to begin with. Kerala is truly one of the most unassuming and breathtaking places to visit in India. You will be left wanting more than you could ever take in through a lifetime. The memories you make here will, without a shadow of a doubt, be some of your absolute best travelling experience.

Serene Chandigarh

Filled with the sight and sweet aroma of over 1,600 different species of roses, the Rose Garden in Chandigarh is a blooming exhibit of nature’s endless beauty and the largest garden of its kind in all of Asia. Officially known as the Zakir Hussain Rose Garden, the garden is named after the former president of India and is spread over an area of 30 acres. The garden also has 32,500 varieties of trees and medicinal shrubs that include the likes of Bel, Bahera, Harar, Camphor, and Yellow Gulmohar. A sight for sore eyes, this attractive garden is decked up with an opulent variety of flowers neatly planted in carved-out lawns and flower beds that are maintained regularly by the Government of Chandigarh. Replete with colorful flowers in every direction and some misty mountains in the backdrop, this garden is the perfect place to relax, which is why it is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Chandigarh, especially among nature lovers.
